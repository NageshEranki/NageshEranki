Project Goal

Hopcopter was inspired by a project of the same name described in the research paper "An agile monopedal hopping quadcopter with synergistic hybrid locomotion" by Bai et al. While Bai and their colleagues exploited hopping as a means to enable aggressive (high lateral accelerations) maneuvers for a quadrotor, this project seeks to instead explore ways to use the leg extension to kick objects on the floor!

Technical Deep Dive

Defining requirements:

I approached this problem by defining some technical requirements:

  • Path planning: The quadrotor, given its current location and the soccerball's location, should be able to track a trajectory that begins from its starting position and ending at the soccerball. This trajectory can comprise of two phases, a lunging phase where it builds momentum, and a free fall phase where it falls towards the soccerball.
  • Attitude control: We would like to point the leg extension towards the ball as it falls down. This was an interesting problem and I found out through experiments, the impact of motor saturation on tracking performance.
  • Landing position control: After gathering enough momentum, when the quadrotor switches to the free fall phase of its flight, we would like to precisely control the landing position. This requirement conflicts with the above requirement (Attitude control), since the applied thrust and torque commands are coupled through the motor mixing algorithm.
There are some smaller, practical subproblems I needed to address to achieve my objective. For example, there is no way to define a rigid body in motion capture software to track soccerballs since any markers pasted on a ball will eventually be hidden as the ball rolls.
