Project Goal

Observations of a OneWeb satellite were made by an amatuer astronomer in Champaign, IL (Latitude: 40.11 deg N, Longitude: -88.24 deg E) on May 7, 2023:

The objective is to determine an initial approximation of the state vector (which comprises the position and velocity with respect to the Earth's center) and then, use statistical orbit determination to improve upon the estimate.

Results and Outcome

The (nonlinear) least squares method was used to determine a best-fit state vector. This method is well suited for nonlinear dynamical systems, such as a satellite orbiting the Earth and morever, seeks to minimize the squared residual error of the estimates with respect to each observation. However, this method is non-recursive and for a large number of observation, will become computationally intensive.

State Vector:

\(\mathbf{x} = [\mathbf{r}, \mathbf{v}]^T\)

where \(\mathbf{r} = [-4.27, -0.94, 6.18]\) and \(\mathbf{v} = [5.87, 0.78, 4.18]\) is the position (km) and velocity (km/s) respectively in the Geocentric Equatorial Frame of reference.
